Chess Principles

Home Page

  1. Develop your pieces quickly.
  2. Try to control the centre.
  3. Pieces first, pawns second.
  4. Do not move the same piece twice in the opening phase.
  5. Loose pieces drop off.
  6. Maintain a good pawn structure.
  7. When the pieces are equal, the player with the better pawn structure wins.
  8. Every pawn is a potential queen.
  9. Castle early.
  10. Do not get your queen out too early in the opening phase.
  11. Rooks belong on open files.
  12. A knight in the rim is dim (grim).
  13. A bishop pair is better than a knight pair or a knight and a bishop.
  14. A Knight is valuable in the middle game where there are closed positions.
  15. A Bishop is valuable in the end game where there are open positions.
  16. Do not exchange your bishop for a knight without purpose.
  17. Centalise your king in the end game.
  18. Winning is a matter of time.
